🧭 How it works

In the roundtable channel you drive the bot by @mentioning it with a keyword. It reads the conversation (and the diplomacy thread for relationship status), writes the report, and posts it to the diplomacy summary channel so everyone on the team has a clean record. Relationships are color-coded 🟢 allies · 🟡 neutral · 🔴 enemies. In other diplomacy/friends channels you can also use the /summary slash command.

🌍 Roundtable commands — @mention the bot

📝 summarize ROUNDTABLE

Purpose
Recap the recent roundtable conversation, with any active scheduled plans and flagged messages attached.
How to use
@mention the bot with the word summarize.
Needs
Access to the roundtable channel.
Example
@Onyx Bot summarize
Result
A short, casual summary posted to the diplomacy summary channel; the bot confirms with a reply pointing you there.
Notes
Only future-dated events are mentioned — anything already past is dropped.

💡 Tips

💡 Getting the best results GOOD TO KNOW

Where it works
The @mention commands work in the roundtable channel; /summary works in other diplomacy/friends channels.
Where it posts
Most reports are posted to the diplomacy summary channel, and the bot replies pointing you there.
Dates
The bot resolves "tomorrow/tonight" against when each message was posted, and only surfaces events still in the future — so old plans won't clutter your reports.
Phrasing
Natural language is fine — it figures out which report you want from your wording (e.g. "give me a recap", "who are our allies?", "any treaties?").
No tools match — try another word.
